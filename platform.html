The Platform


The Brain Simulation Platform is divided into three main sections: Online Use Cases, Model Collabs and Open Source Tools. Please refer to the following sections for a brief introduction and to learn about other tools that help the user in their experience with the Platform.

Online Use Cases

The Online Use Cases enable you to work through a number of pipelines; these are categorised by level of expertise and by their service maturity level. This section also highlights the use cases under development.

Browse the Online Use Cases

Model Collabs

The Model Collabs section has a number of Collabs illustrating the scaffold models of rodent brain regions; these include:

  • basal ganglia
  • cerebellum
  • hippocampus
  • somatosensory cortex
There are also Collabs on subcellular modelling, human neuron models and a point-neuron cortical microcircuit. You can also find a useful description of ‘what is a scaffold model’ and how we will transition from scaffold models to more community-driven models.

Open the model collabs

Open source tools

Key software developed and integral to the Platform can be found in this section. Many of the applications, such as the simulators NEST, STEPS, NEURON, or tools for analysing morphologies and optimising parameters, can be accessed directly or through the Software Catalog.

Your own Collab/community

In addition to the tools and services provided by the Brain Simulation Platform, you can create your own Collab for working collaboratively on a project and include services provided by the Brain Simulation or other HBP Platforms. You can invite members to join your Collab. Access to privileged services may be subject to conditions and further approval.
